Škoda RegioPanter

Creation and Development

Škoda Transportation, a subsidiary of the PPF Group, began producing the RegioPanter series in 2011. The RegioPanter was designed as a modern electric multiple unit (EMU) for regional and suburban rail services, aiming to enhance passenger comfort and accessibility.

Features and Specifications

Available in two to six-car units, with lengths ranging from 52.9 meters for two-car units to 159.2 meters for six-car units. Depending on the configuration, the RegioPanter can accommodate between 147 to 400 passengers. The train can reach speeds of up to 160 km/h on electrified tracks and 120 km/h on battery power. The latest models include battery technology allowing for zero-emission operation on non-electrified sections, making them an eco-friendly alternative to diesel trains.

Current Status

As of 2024, Czech Railways plans to deploy four battery-powered RegioPanter trains in the Moravian-Silesian Region. This initiative marks a significant step towards sustainable rail transport in the region. Bulgaria has also contracted Škoda Group for up to 25 barrier-free RegioPanter EMUs, with a firm order for 20 units, set to replace outdated rolling stock and improve passenger services.
